History
Joseph
Müller-Brockmann
1914/1966
Was always drawing in school
Teacher noticed
Suggested he pursue artistic career
“I became a graphic designer by accident… At school, I didn’t like writing so much so I started drawing.”
Became apprentice retoucher in a printing house (only lasted a day because it wasn’t artistic enough)
Then apprenticed to two old architects
Architecture, Design and History of art at University of Zurich and Zurich University of the Arts
Apprenticeship to designer and advertising consultant Walter Diggleman
Accomplishments:
Career:
Designed for poster in 1950 for the Tonhalle in Zurich -- started developing his constructivist approach to graphic design
1957: Professor of Graphic design at the Kunstgewerbeschule, Zurich
Co-editor of Trilingual journal focused on swiss design from 1958 to 1965
Became IBM European design consultant in 1967
Founded the communication agency Muller-Brockmann & Co.
